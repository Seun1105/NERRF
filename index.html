About the Initiative

Building infrastructure that respects what already exists

The National Environmental Risk Reduction Framework (NERRF) is a forward-looking initiative focused on improving the efficiency, consistency, and reliability of environmental planning and compliance processes across the United States.

Environmental review and permitting systems are critical to sustainable infrastructure development. Yet current processes often face challenges — inconsistent risk evaluation, fragmented coordination, and compliance workflow bottlenecks.

NERRF addresses these challenges with structured, scalable methodologies that enhance environmental risk identification and decision-making at the implementation level — where projects are planned, evaluated, and executed.

The Problem

Planning systems that are essential — yet falling short

Environmental planning across federally regulated projects is critical — but systemic inefficiencies create cascading risks for communities, ecosystems, and budgets.

Key Challenges

  • Late-Stage Risk Discovery

    Environmental risks identified after planning is underway force costly rework and timeline extensions.

  • Inconsistent Methodologies

    Variable evaluation approaches produce unpredictable outcomes across agencies and project types.

  • Fragmented Coordination

    Siloed agency workflows and disconnected stakeholder communication slow infrastructure delivery.

  • Unsystematic Cumulative Risk

    No unified method for evaluating compounding environmental impacts across projects and geographies.

The Downstream Cost

Delays in infrastructure delivery
Increased project costs
Elevated environmental & public health risks
